Team Introduction

We're a budding robotics team based out of Colorado Springs. Since our inception in 2015, we've grown steadily and ammased years of experience participating in 4 different VEX gamemodes.

What is Vex Robotics?

VEX EDR is a robotics competition for middle school and high school students with two different divisions: middle school and high school. In this division, aluminum and steel parts are used, and the robots are built using the VEX EDR Design System.

While the visual code editor is still available, most students take advantage of VEX's RobotC for their programming. Teams progress through different levels of competitions before culminating at the international event.

How will the team use these funds?

We will be releasing a seasonal financial report in Fall 2018 that asseses our financial needs for the upcoming 2018-2019 school year. The report will feature a detailed list of parts and other expenses we plan on incurring.

Who should the donation be addressed to?

Doherty High School runs a stand-alone fund for the Robotics Team. By check, the payment can be addressed to Doherty High School Robotics. If you wish to make a direct cash donation, please call (719) 930-2257 or email spartanrobotics@gmail.com

Will my donation comply with VEX's team management policy?

VEX provides a detailed compliance policy on their website. You can rest assured that both Doherty High School and our team advisor, Adam Truex, have setup the fund in accordance with their policies.

What will I receive in exchange for my donation?

We offer our donors a number of options to show our gratitude for their support. Advertisers will have their logo featured on our web page, social media accounts, T-shirts, and/or directly on the robot itself.

Who is in charge of managing the team's financials?

Our financials are kept track of and condensed into a report by our team advisor, Adam Truex. His contact info can be found directly on the Doherty High School website.
